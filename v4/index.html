BIOSEQ: Bioinformatics Inquiry through Sequencing

Program Details

The primary objective for the BIOSEQ project is to provide students with a genuine research experience. Specific grant activities include establishing a core sequencing facility for educational purposes at Tufts’ Medford campus, creating an entry level research-based bioinformatics course for high school students, developing curricular units to describe bioinformatics concepts, and continuing Professor Walt’s long time focus on outreach to local K-12 students and teachers in the area of science education.

Faculty supervision: Tufts students who participate in undergraduate research will be mentored by a graduate student and Postdocs as well as an undergraduate who has some experience in the laboratory. Faculty supervision will come in the form of lab meetings.

Site requirements: High schools that wish to participate should have basic laboratory facilities such as blacktop benches, working sinks, gas lines and a fume hood. In the process of scheduling your project we will discuss basic equipment. In most cases, we can provide necessary equipment.

Interested Undergrads

Tufts students who are interested in participating should contact Project Administrator Karen O'Hagan. Students should be prepared to submit an unofficial transcript, a resume, and the name of a science professor who can be contacted as a reference.

Interested High Schools

Teachers who participate in the project should be prepared to do some extra reading and some team teaching on the subjects we cover. Our aim is to have teachers comfortable enough with the project to be able to do it themselves, given the materials.
